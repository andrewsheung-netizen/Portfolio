## Anti-Patterns Verdict

Not AI slop. Deterministic scan: 0 findings (CLI over index.html + src). Committed dark-teal instrument-panel identity, enforced design system, no generic tells.

## What's Working

1. Every destructive action is now reversible or guarded: import (staged+safety-export), erase (two-step+safety-export), sell/close (undo ledger), delete (toast-undo). The product no longer has a one-click way to lose data.
2. The silence-is-a-feature principle now holds on the answer block too: a flat day shows "Flat since yesterday" with no zero-movers clutter.
3. Status honesty: the topbar states "prices manual" rather than stamping a fresh-looking time on a refresh that fetched nothing.

## Priority Issues

All remaining items are P3 power-user reach; no P0/P1/P2 outstanding.

- [P3] No column sorting, ticker->name/price autofill on Add, or bulk actions. First wants as the book grows past one screen.
- [P3] No explicit retry button on a failed price refresh (the R hotkey/button is the implicit path; failures surface in the action strip).
- [P3] No first-class shortcuts/help reference (hotkeys live only in button tooltips).

## Persona Red Flags

- Alex (power user): daily ritual is fast (hotkeys, Add & next, toggles). Remaining: sorting/autofill/bulk for a growing book.
- Sam (screen reader/keyboard): labeled controls, aria-pressed tabs, aria-live confirms+toast, focus-first-field, guarded dismissal. Strong.
- Riley (stress tester): all destructive paths guarded/reversible; double-submit, future-date, qty-0 blocked; flat-day and clock-rollover handled cleanly. Hard to break.

## Minor Observations

- Activity caps at 8 rows, no in-app "show all" (backups hold the rest).
- Light theme deferred (tokens structured for it).
- Undo toast is single-slot (a second delete supersedes the first's undo) — acceptable for the use pattern.
