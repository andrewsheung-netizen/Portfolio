## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment: not AI slop.** No side-stripes, no gradient text, no glassmorphism, no metric-card grid (the Home card is the only card), normal-case labels, committed teal rather than cream-default, purpose-written microcopy. A Linear/Stripe-fluent user would mostly trust it; the pause-points are small (sun-like Settings glyph, sell sheet titled "Edit equity", state-vs-action toggle label).

**Deterministic scan: 1 finding.** `layout-transition` at src/styles/app.css:301 — `.alloc-seg { transition: width }` animates a layout property, and it genuinely fires (AllocationBand sets inline percent widths). Real but minor: a 10px bar with ≤5 segments. Polish item, not a defect. Browser overlay injection ran in the live page and reported the identical single finding; CLI and in-page detectors agree. No false positives. Notably the detector caught the one thing the design review missed, and the design review caught everything the detector can't see (a11y semantics, destructive-flow gaps).

## Overall Impression

This is a coherent, disciplined instrument that does not look or feel AI-generated — the design system is actually enforced in the shipped CSS, and the sell→activity→undo loop is real product thinking. Its weaknesses are concentrated in one theme: **the last mile of trust and efficiency for its single expert user** — the most destructive action (import) skips the system's own two-step rule, the attention strip names actions it won't perform, AT semantics are half-finished, and a daily-use cockpit has no keyboard paths. The single biggest opportunity: make "what moved today and why" answerable below the headline.

## What's Working

1. **The design system is enforced, not aspirational** — no shadows anywhere, every numeral mono, cyan only on interactives, the attention strip truly absent when clean. Rare DESIGN.md ↔ shipped-CSS fidelity.
2. **The sell/close flow** — atomic transaction, proceeds routed to chosen cash, realized P&L recorded, weighted-average undo with human error messages. The live preview before commit is exactly the right reassurance at the highest-stakes frequent action.
3. **Honest data states** — unpriced positions excluded from totals *and* flagged, "since yesterday" computed against a real prior snapshot, missing-FX asterisk, "—" never fake zeros.

## Priority Issues

- **[P0] Import replaces all data the instant a file is picked** (Settings.tsx → importAll clears every table). One mis-click destroys the entire financial record; violates the system's own "destructive actions are never one click" rule at the single most destructive point. **Fix:** confirm step with bundle summary (exportedAt, per-table counts) + auto-download a safety export of current data first. *Suggested: /impeccable harden*
- **[P1] Interactive semantics are invisible to AT**: ledger rows are bare `tr[tabindex=0]` with no role/label (Enter-to-edit is a secret), kind-tabs use role="tab" without arrow-key behavior, Undo/Delete confirm-swaps have no aria-live. **Fix:** real button semantics in the ticker cell ("Edit NVDA"), plain buttons + aria-pressed for type tabs, aria-live="polite" on confirm buttons. *Suggested: /impeccable harden (a11y) or /impeccable audit*
- **[P1] Attention chips are inert** for expiry/stale-property (only unpriced/no-key get actions). The strip names the action then makes the user scroll and hunt. **Fix:** chip click opens the relevant edit sheet — the flag already carries the object. *Suggested: /impeccable polish*
- **[P2] "What moved today" unanswered below the headline**: P&L is lifetime unrealized, no per-position day change, mobile hides P&L entirely. The user sees −HK$773 with no attribution. **Fix:** day-change column (or P&L↔Day toggle) from prior snapshot prices; surface top movers. *Suggested: /impeccable shape day-change attribution (small) then build*
- **[P2] Sheet polish cluster**: sell/close keeps "Edit equity" title; focus lands on the Close button (first Enter dismisses); demo data is a one-way door (no "erase everything"). **Fix:** dynamic titles ("Sell VOO"), focus first field, two-step "Erase all data" in Settings. *Suggested: /impeccable polish*

## Persona Red Flags

**Alex (impatient power user):** no hotkeys at all (no `a` add, `r` refresh, `/` find); inert expiry chip costs four steps for the most predictable daily action; adding 10 positions = opening the sheet 10 times; "as of" implies freshness after keyless refresh; no column sorting.

**Sam (screen reader / keyboard):** row-edit undiscoverable (announces as a plain table row); sheets open with focus on Close — first Enter dismisses; Undo→Confirm mutation is silent and onBlur resets it mid-investigation. Bright spots: labeled sparkline/allocation (role="img"), aria-live status, labeled icon buttons, visible focus rings.

**Riley (stress tester):** wrong backup file = instant total replacement; double-click on "Sell VOO" can write the trade twice (submit not disabled during async); future sale dates and qty-0 positions accepted. Handled well: qty>held blocked with a human message, missing FX degrades to "—"+flag, undo against deleted cash throws an actionable error.

## Minor Observations

- Settings gear reads as a sun/brightness glyph (theme-toggle confusion).
- Mortgage balance in loss-red stretches Semantic Quarantine (debt ≠ performance direction).
- Activity rows show native-currency realized while the YTD header is HKD — both labeled, but mixed in one panel.
- `*` on YTD realized explained nowhere.
- "today" age-note repeats under every manual price — noise when everything is manual.
- Undo re-inserts positions at the bottom of the ledger (order instability).
- `.alloc-seg` width transition (detector finding) — transform/FLIP would be cleaner.
- tokens.css promises a light twin that doesn't exist yet; Activity caps at 8 with no in-app way to see older trades.

## Questions to Consider

1. The headline promises "what moved today" — but everything below the fold answers "what have I made since purchase." Is the ledger serving yesterday's question instead of the daily one?
2. The system's proudest rule is the two-step confirm — why is the most destructive act in the product (import-replace-everything) the only one exempt?
3. For one expert pilot opening this twice a day: where's the muscle memory? Right now, is this an instrument panel — or a beautifully typeset daily report?
