# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A single user: Andrew, the owner-operator. He checks this daily — quick glances from his phone (markets open, evening check-ins) and longer sessions at a desktop. He is financially literate; no concepts need dumbing down. There is no second persona: no clients, no recruiters, no onboarding funnel.

## Product Purpose

A live personal portfolio and net-worth tracker. It unifies investment holdings (equities, funds, cash) with real-world assets and liabilities — notably the mortgage — into one picture of net worth and performance. Success looks like: open the app, and within seconds know (1) what my net worth is, (2) what moved today and why, (3) whether anything needs action. It replaces scattered brokerage apps and spreadsheets as the single daily source of truth.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, energetic, decisive. Opening it should feel like settling into a cockpit — fast, high-contrast, alive with market motion — not filing into a records room. The energy comes from responsiveness, crisp hierarchy, and confident data presentation, never from clutter or gimmicks. Three words: **sharp, kinetic, assured**.

## Anti-references

- **Bloomberg terminal clutter**: no wall-to-wall data density, no intimidating chrome, not every pixel filled. Energy ≠ noise.
- **Spreadsheet aesthetic**: no gridlines-everywhere, flat-hierarchy "Excel in a browser" feel. Tables must have typographic hierarchy, not just rows.
- (Implicit) Generic SaaS dashboard scaffolding — cookie-cutter metric cards and template grids — would also undercut the single-owner, purpose-built character.

## Design Principles

1. **Answer in three seconds.** Net worth, today's move, and anything actionable are legible before a single scroll or click. Everything else is one level down.
2. **Energy without clutter.** Sharpness comes from contrast, motion, and pace — not from cramming. Every number on screen earns its place; the rest waits behind interaction.
3. **One owner, zero ceremony.** No marketing chrome, no explanatory hand-holding, no empty-state sales pitches. The interface assumes its one expert user and gets out of the way.
4. **The whole picture, not just the brokerage.** Investments and real-world assets/liabilities (mortgage) live in one model. Net worth is the spine; portfolio performance is a chapter of it.
5. **Pocket-first glances, desk-deep sessions.** The phone view is a first-class quick-read, not a squeezed desktop. Depth (history, breakdowns, what-ifs) belongs to the desktop session.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA baseline: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, full keyboard operability.
- Fully responsive: phone quick-check and desktop deep-dive are both primary contexts.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` — the "kinetic" personality must degrade to a calm equivalent.
- Don't encode gain/loss in color alone; pair red/green with sign, shape, or direction.
